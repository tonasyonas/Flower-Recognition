\begin{abstract}
Few-shot image classification remains a fundamental challenge in computer vision, particularly for fine-grained tasks where inter-class differences are subtle and intra-class variations are substantial. This project presents a systematic evaluation of advanced convolutional neural network (CNN) architectures and Visual Prompt Tuning (VPT) for few-shot flower classification on the Oxford Flowers 102 dataset.

We evaluate eleven ResNet18-based configurations, a ResNet50 baseline, and two VPT variants (Shallow and Deep) across $k \in \{1, 2, 5, 10\}$-shot training regimes, using mean per-class accuracy as our primary metric following \citet{nilsback2008}. Contrary to common expectations, advanced convolutional modifications (dilated, deformable, depthwise-separable, and a novel hybrid) consistently underperform a standard ResNet18 baseline. Regularization helps modestly: freezing the first two ResNet18 layers reaches 90.82\% mean per-class accuracy on the test set, while MixUp surprisingly hurts performance under such extreme data scarcity. VPT-Deep reaches 92.83\% mean per-class accuracy while training only 0.2\% of the backbone parameters, outperforming every CNN configuration we tried. Few-shot results show the gap between VPT-Deep and full fine-tuning widens as training data shrinks, supporting the view that parameter-efficient tuning is the right tool for fine-grained few-shot classification.
\end{abstract}
