\subsection{Triplet Loss + VPT-Deep}

The project handout suggests triplet loss as an advanced loss function. We add a batch-hard triplet loss \citep{schroff2015facenet} on the L2-normalised CLS embeddings of VPT-Deep, with margin $0.5$ and weight $\alpha = 0.2$. The combined loss is $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{CE}} + \alpha \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{triplet}}$.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\toprule
Method & Test Acc (\%) & MPC Acc (\%) & Best Val Acc (\%) \\
\midrule
VPT-Deep                    & 91.49 & 92.69 & 93.63 \\
VPT-Deep + Triplet ($\alpha{=}0.2$) & 91.49 & 92.69 & \textbf{94.02} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Triplet loss raises validation accuracy by 0.4 points but does not change test-set numbers, suggesting the batch-hard triplet objective overfits slightly to the validation distribution.}
\label{tab:triplet}
\end{table}

We initially observed NaN losses when triplet was added on top of mixed-precision training; L2-normalising the embeddings before computing pairwise distances and computing the triplet term in float32 outside the autocast region resolved this.
